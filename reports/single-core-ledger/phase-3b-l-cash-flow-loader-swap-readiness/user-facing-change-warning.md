# User-facing change warning — Cash Flow loader swap (future)

**Current status:** Legacy Cash Flow is still the **official** report. **No change has been made.**

## If a loader swap is approved and executed in a future phase

Cash Flow totals **may change** for DIN CHINA and DIN BRIDAL because finance approved a **different accounting basis** for the unified report:

- **Internal transfers** are excluded from normal Cash Flow totals (finance rule Q5=C). The legacy report still shows transfer legs as cash-in/cash-out.
- **Opening balance rows** are summary-only, not period cash-in (finance rule Q4=A). The legacy report may count some opening activity as period cash-in.

DIN COUTURE is expected to show **little or no change** because aligned preview already matches legacy at summary level.

## What does NOT change

- Journal entries, payments, stock, and balances are **not modified** by switching which report engine displays Cash Flow.
- Only **how the Cash Flow screen calculates and displays** period totals changes.
- Other reports (Roznamcha, Party Ledger, Trial Balance, etc.) are unaffected unless separately approved.

## Approval required

A loader swap requires **separate written operator approval** in addition to finance basis sign-off. Until then, the legacy Cash Flow report remains what users see by default.

## Plain-language summary for staff

> "We are not changing your data. If approved later, the Cash Flow report may show different totals because we agreed on new finance rules for how transfers and opening balances appear. The old report stays in place until leadership explicitly approves the switch."
